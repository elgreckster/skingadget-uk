FAQ

Quick answers to the things people ask right before they buy.

Should I start with an LED mask or microcurrent?

If you want the lowest-effort routine, start with LED. If you’re happy to do a slightly fiddlier routine with conductive gel, microcurrent can be a good choice — but only if you’ll use it consistently.

How often should I use these devices?

Follow the manufacturer’s instructions. As a general habit rule, it’s better to do a short routine a few times a week than a long routine once and then give up.

How long until I notice results?

Expect changes to be gradual. If you’re not using it consistently for several weeks, it’s too early to judge — and if you get irritation, stop and reassess.

What matters most when choosing an LED mask?

Comfort and coverage (you’ll actually wear it), a clear return policy, and sensible safety guidance (especially around the eyes). The “most powerful” marketing rarely beats a device you’ll use 3× per week.

Do I need conductive gel for microcurrent?

Usually, yes. Most microcurrent devices work best with a conductive gel or serum recommended by the manufacturer; skipping it can reduce performance and may feel unpleasant.

Is it safe for sensitive skin?

Sensitive skin varies. Start gently, keep sessions short, and stop if you get redness that doesn’t settle. If you have a diagnosed condition or you’re unsure, speak to a qualified professional.

Who should avoid these devices?

Always read the manufacturer’s contraindications. In general, be cautious if you’re pregnant, have epilepsy, have an implanted medical device (for microcurrent), or take medication that makes you light-sensitive (for LED), and ask a clinician if in doubt.

Can I return a device after opening it?

Returns depend on the seller and hygiene rules. Before buying, check the UK listing’s returns policy and whether the item is treated as a personal-care product once opened.
