LED vs microcurrent (quick version)

Honestly? Pick the one you’ll actually use. Both work if you’re consistent. Neither works if they live in a drawer.

LED: set it and forget it

  • Effort level: Low. Strap it on, hit start, scroll your phone for 10 minutes.
  • Ongoing cost: Zero (once you’ve bought it).
  • Best for: People who want results without thinking too hard.

Microcurrent: slightly fiddly

  • Effort level: Medium. You need gel, a mirror, and 5–10 mins of actual focus.
  • Ongoing cost: £10/month for gel (approx).
  • Best for: People who enjoy routines and don’t mind the faff.

FAQ (honest answers)

The stuff people actually want to know before hitting “buy now.”

LED or microcurrent — which one should I start with?

LED if you want low-effort. Microcurrent if you don’t mind a slightly fiddlier routine with gel. But honestly? Whichever one you’ll actually use consistently is the right answer.

How often do I need to use these?

Follow what the manual says, but realistically: a short routine 3× a week beats an hour-long session once and then giving up forever.

When will I see results?

Weeks, not days. If you’re expecting Instagram-filter results by Friday, you’re going to be disappointed. And if your skin gets irritated, stop immediately.

What’s actually important when choosing an LED mask?

Comfort (will you wear it?), coverage (does it actually cover your face?), and a returns policy that doesn’t require a lawyer. “Most powerful” marketing means nothing if you use it twice.

Do I really need conductive gel for microcurrent?

Yes. Don’t try to wing it with moisturiser or water. It won’t work properly and it’ll feel weird. Buy the gel.

Is this safe for sensitive skin?

Depends on your skin. Start gentle, keep sessions short, and if you get redness that doesn’t settle, stop. If you’ve got a diagnosed condition or you’re not sure, ask your GP.

Who shouldn’t use these?

Check the manufacturer’s warnings, but generally: pregnant, epileptic, have a pacemaker (microcurrent), or take light-sensitive meds (LED). When in doubt, ask a professional, not Reddit.

Can I return it after opening the box?

Depends on the seller. Some treat it like a personal-care item (no returns once opened). Check the listing’s return policy before you buy, especially if it’s £150+.
