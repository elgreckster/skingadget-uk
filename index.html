Who should skip IPL

Important Read this
  • Dark skin tones: IPL doesn’t work safely on skin tones V–VI (Fitzpatrick scale). The light can’t distinguish hair from skin, risking burns.
  • Light/grey/red hair: IPL needs dark pigment (melanin) to work. Blonde, ginger, grey, or white hair won’t respond.
  • Pregnant/epileptic: Check contraindications. Most brands say no.
  • Alternative: For darker skin or light hair, look at professional laser (diode/Nd:YAG) or electrolysis instead.

Reality check: IPL takes 8–12 weeks to see real results. You need to use it weekly at first, then monthly for maintenance. If you can’t commit to that schedule, save your money.

Do you even need one?

Honest talk
  • Skip if: You already have a solid skincare routine and don’t need extra steps. A hot flannel does 70% of the job for free.
  • Get one if: You love rituals, have congested pores, or want better serum absorption. Also great before clay masks.

Verdict: Steamers are a luxury, not a necessity. If £27–£100 feels like “why not,” go for it. If you’re on a tight budget, skip and spend on actives instead (retinol, niacinamide, vitamin C).

Gua sha vs roller?

Quick comparison
  • Gua sha: Better for sculpting, releasing tension, targeting specific areas (jawline, cheekbones). Requires technique (watch a YouTube tutorial first).
  • Jade roller: Easier to use (just roll), better for general de-puffing and product absorption. Less targeted.
  • Verdict: If you’re lazy or new, get a roller. If you want sculpting and don’t mind learning proper technique, get gua sha. Or get both in a set.

Reality check: These work for puffiness and product absorption, but won’t “contour your face” like surgery. Keep in fridge, use with serum or oil, and do it daily for best results. 2 minutes in the morning = noticeable difference in under-eye bags.

FAQ (honest answers)

The stuff people actually want to know before hitting “buy now.” No fluff, just straight answers.

Is any of this actually worth it, or is it all marketing hype?

Mix of both. LED and microcurrent have clinical backing (decades of studies). IPL genuinely works if you match the right skin/hair combo. Gua sha and steamers are lower-tech but effective for what they do (depuffing, pore prep). The hype is in the “instant miracle” claims — everything here takes weeks of consistency. If you can’t commit to that, save your money.

Which one should I buy first?

Depends on your main gripe. Acne/redness? Start with LED. Puffy face/saggy jawline? Microcurrent or gua sha. Sick of shaving? IPL. Want a relaxing ritual? Steamer or jade roller. Don’t buy everything at once — you’ll use none of them.

How often do I need to use these?

Follow the manual, but generally: LED 3–5×/week, microcurrent 3–5×/week, IPL weekly for 8–12 weeks then monthly maintenance, steamers 2–3×/week, gua sha daily (2 mins). Short and consistent beats long and sporadic every time.

When will I see results?

LED/microcurrent: 4–8 weeks. IPL: 6–12 weeks. Gua sha/steamers: immediate for puffiness, 4+ weeks for texture. If you’re expecting magic by day 3, you’ll be disappointed. Stick with it or don’t bother.

Can I return these after opening?

Amazon’s UK return policy is usually 30 days, even if opened (as long as it’s not damaged). Check the specific listing — some third-party sellers have stricter rules. Always test within the return window.

Do I really need the expensive versions?

Not always. Sensse LED mask (£140) does 80% of what £450 masks do. BeautyPro microcurrent (£39) tested similarly to NuFACE (£385). But for IPL, the £200+ devices (Philips, Braun) genuinely outperform £80 knockoffs in power and safety. Spend on what matters for your use case.

What about counterfeits and fakes?

Stick to “Ships from and sold by Amazon UK” or verified brands. Avoid “too good to be true” prices (£50 for a “Philips Lumea” = fake). Check reviews for mentions of authenticity. If in doubt, buy from Boots/Argos/direct brand sites.

Who should avoid these devices?

Pregnant: skip microcurrent, IPL, most LED (check contraindications). Epileptic: avoid flashing lights (LED, IPL). Pacemaker: no microcurrent. Dark skin tones (V–VI): most IPL unsafe (burns risk). Light/grey/ginger hair: IPL won’t work. When in doubt, ask your GP, not TikTok.
